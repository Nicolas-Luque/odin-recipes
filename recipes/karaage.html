Karaage (Japanese Fried Chicken)

Description

Karaage is a popular Japanese dish of bite-sized marinated and lightly battered fried chicken pieces. The chicken is seasoned with a flavorful combination of soy sauce, ginger, and garlic before being coated in a crispy layer of potato starch or flour. The result is juicy, tender, and irresistibly delicious fried chicken.

Often served as a snack, appetizer, or even as a main dish with rice, karaage is a versatile and crowd-pleasing favorite that's enjoyed by all ages.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless chicken thighs (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 2 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 Tbsp potato starch (or cornstarch)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Chopped green onions (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix soy sauce, sake, grated ginger, and minced garlic to make the marinade.
  2. Add chicken pieces to the marinade, coating them evenly. Let marinate for at least 15-30 minutes.
  3. Sprinkle potato starch over the marinated chicken, ensuring each piece is coated.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan. Fry the chicken in batches until golden brown and cooked through.
  5. Place fried chicken on a paper towel to drain excess oil.
  6. Garnish with chopped green onions and serve with lemon wedges.
  7. Enjoy the crispy and flavorful karaage!
