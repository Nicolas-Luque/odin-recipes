Takoyaki

Description

Takoyaki is a popular Japanese street food that features savory octopus-filled dough balls, cooked until crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. These bite-sized treats are a delightful blend of textures and flavors, often enjoyed with toppings like takoyaki sauce, mayonnaise, bonito flakes, and seaweed.

Originating in Osaka, takoyaki has become a beloved snack across Japan and is a must-try for anyone exploring Japanese cuisine. With its charming round shape and unique taste, it's no wonder that takoyaki stands are a common sight at festivals and food markets.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1¾ cups dashi stock
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp tenkasu (crunchy bits of tempura batter)
  • 2 green onions (finely chopped)
  • 1 cup cooked octopus (cut into small pieces)
  • Takoyaki sauce
  • Mayonnaise
  • Aonori (seaweed flakes)
  • Bonito flakes

Steps

  1. In a bowl, whisk together flour, eggs, dashi stock, soy sauce, salt, and baking powder until smooth.
  2. Preheat a takoyaki pan and brush each mold with oil. Pour batter into each mold, filling them halfway.
  3. Add a sprinkle of tenkasu and chopped green onions into each mold.
  4. Place a piece of cooked octopus into each mold. Pour more batter to cover the filling.
  5. As the edges cook, use a skewer or chopsticks to turn the takoyaki balls 90 degrees. Continue cooking and turning until all sides are golden brown and crispy.
  6. Remove takoyaki from the pan and place on a plate. Drizzle with takoyaki sauce and mayonnaise.
  7. Sprinkle with aonori and bonito flakes.
  8. Serve the delicious takoyaki while hot!
